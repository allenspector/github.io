What is Linux and Why should I learn it?

Linux is Ubiquitious

From smartphones to cars, to supercomputers and home appliances, to home desktops and to enterprise servers, the Linux operating system is everywhere. It's used in cell phones, in thermostats, in cars, in refrigerators, Roku devices, in televisons, the Mars Rover, and so on. In fact, Linux is so powerful that Linux runs most of the Internet, having the majority of web servers use Linux, not only in the United States but also in the rest of the world. Additionally, the operating system is used in the world’s top 500 supercomputers, and in the world’s stock exchanges, and in the hospitality industry, and in the airline industry, and in the medical industry, and in many embedded devices too, these devices commonly referred to as the Internet of Things.

Just like Windows and Apple, Linux can be run as a desktop, as a server, and as an embedded device, this operating system having been described as the most reliable, secure, and worry-free operating system available.

In a nutshell, you should learn Linux it's free and opensource, and there is a thriving community of people willng to help, open source software being driven by communities of passionate and highly skilled individuals--with Linux being probably the most popular of them all. And most notably in this community, there are a great number of great jobs, making Linux a desirable skills for technologists and non-technologists.

May the FORCE--or Open Source be with you

Open source software is software that is designed to be publicly accessible—anyone can see, modify, and distribute the programming code.

Open source projects are a part of our daily lives. You might be reading this article on your cell phone or tablet, and if so, you are likely using open source technology right now. The operating systems for both iPhone and Android were originally created using building blocks from open source software, projects, and programs. If you are reading this article on your laptop or desktop, are you using Chrome or Firefox as the web browser? Mozilla Firefox is an open source web browser. Google Chrome is a modified version of the open-source browser project called Chromium — though Chromium was started by Google developers who continue to play an active role in the updating and additional development, Google has added programming and features (some of which are not open source) to this base software to develop the Google Chrome browser. Still companies are lookhng at open source as a way to produce the best and most reliable software, making Linux an important skill and making open source a more viable alternative for companies.

The Linux Mascot

by AVID Staff Reporter

Back in 1996, several members of a Linux group were chatting about a suitable logo and mascot for the new Linux operating system. At the time, many of the suggestions were nothing more than parodies of other operating system's logos. Other suggestions that were tosssed around were more related to nature, such as sharks and eagles. It wasn't until Linus Torvalds (the creator of Linux) mentioned that he was fond of penguins. That mention pretty much ended the debate and a penguin would become the mascot of Linux. And he is called Tux.
